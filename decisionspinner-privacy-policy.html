Decision Spinner - Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Decision Spinner by Tristonia (“we”, “our”, or “us”) handles your information when you use our mobile application.

Last Updated: 11 July 2026

Decision Spinner does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Everything you add to the app stays on your device.

1. About Decision Spinner

Decision Spinner is a spinning-wheel decision-making app. There is no account, no sign-in, and no server component — the app works entirely offline aside from one font request described below.

2. Information We Collect

None. We do not collect any personal data, usage data, or device identifiers. There is no analytics or advertising SDK in the app.

3. Data Storage

Your options, spin history, and settings are stored locally on your device using Android’s DataStore. This data is never transmitted anywhere, including to us, and is not accessible to any third party. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data.

4. Permissions

Decision Spinner requests two device permissions:

  • Vibrate: used for haptic feedback while the wheel spins.
  • Internet: used solely to fetch the display typefaces used by each wheel theme via Google’s downloadable-fonts service (see below). No personal or app data is sent with this request.

5. Third-Party Services

The only network call the app makes is to Google’s downloadable-fonts service, which supplies the fonts used by the app’s wheel themes. This is a standard Android system request made on the app’s behalf and does not include any personal or usage data. Decision Spinner does not use any analytics, advertising, or tracking services.

6. Data Sharing

We don’t share data with third parties, because we don’t collect any to share.

7. Children’s Privacy

Decision Spinner is rated for a general audience (Everyone) and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

If we update this Privacy Policy, the changes will be posted on this page with a revised “Last Updated” date.
